# Lifting the robot {% tag="lifting_the_robot" %}

Suspended robots may fall during lifting. Personnel risk serious crush injury.

  * Never pass under or extend any limbs under a suspended robot.

  * Always utilize sufficient support if you need to lift the robot off the ground while performing maintenance, so that the robot is fixed in place and cannot fall, shift, or slip.

To lift the robot, follow the steps below.

  1. Remove any top module currently mounted to the robot. See the manual for your robot application for instructions on removing either MiR Shelf Carrier 250 or MiR Hook 250 top modules.

  2. Turn the two screws counterclockwise on the side cover, and remove the cover from the robot. Use a T30 bit. This will allow the robot to rest on support blocks, without resting on the side covers.

<image: "side\_unscrew.png">

  3. Remove the four M8 mounting bolts in the top plate of the robot. Use a T45 bit. Tighten the bolts to 15 Nm when remounting them (this torque may vary depending on whether a top module is attached).

<image: "remove\_top\_mounting\_screws\_side\_cover\_off.png">

  4. Mount four M8 × 16 mm eye-bolts to the mounting holes. Make sure to screw the eye-bolts all the way in. Do not tighten the bolts more than 7.5 Nm. The eye bolts should be oriented in parallel to the opposite diagonal bolt to ensure stable lifting.

<image: "attach\_eye\_bolts\_side\_cover\_off.png">

  5. Use an appropriate lifting device to lift the robot. The lifting device should be capable of lifting at least 400 kg.

<image: "lift\_with\_harness\_top\_side\_covers\_off.png">

  6. Place the robot on a sturdy work bench, or similar, capable of supporting the total weight of the robot, and place the robot onto wooden support blocks. Make sure to place the wooden blocks so that they support the frame of the robot on both sides.

<image: "mir250\_supported\_wooden\_blocks.png">
