# Check the caster wheels {% tag="check_the_caster_wheels" %}

<image: "mir250\_maintenance\_caster\_wheels.png">

## Purpose

To ensure that any defects are discovered before potentially causing issues.

When checking the caster wheels on the robot, note that the caster wheel brace has a little play in the way it is attached to the robot frame. This is intended design.

Frequency: **half-yearly**

To complete this task, the robot does not need to be powered.

Turn off the robot, and disconnect the battery.

  * When performing maintenance on robots, you risk electrical shock from electrical wires and connectors if internal components are exposed, crushing, collision, or pinching from unexpected movements.

## Procedure

On MiR250, the caster wheels are exposed and can be checked without removing any parts.

  1. Press against the caster wheel bearing while rotating the wheel to feel for any internal wear or damage. Listen for any grinding noises. Check all four caster wheel bearings.

  2. Visually check the caster wheels' condition. If the center seam is worn away, it is a sign that the caster wheel needs to be replaced soon.

  3. Check that there are no flat spots on the caster wheels.

  4. Measure the diameter of the caster wheel to determine the actual wear. Use a vernier caliper or measuring tape. Verify that the diameter is no less than 122 mm. For cleanroom wheels, verify that the diameter is no less than 123 mm.

See a [video](https://vimeo.com/896453650) of the process on the Mobile Industrial Robots profile on vimeo.com.

## Pass criteria <image: "mmg\_pass.svg">

  * The caster wheel bearings have no damage or exhibit grinding noises.

  * The caster wheels are within tolerance of at least 122 mm in diameter. For cleanroom wheels, the minimum diameter is 123 mm.

  * The caster wheels have no flat spots.

## Remediation

{% table %}

 * Issue
 * Remedial action
 ---
 * Caster wheel bearing is exhibiting a grinding noise or is spinning unevenly when the caster wheel is rotated.
 * Replace all four caster wheels. See How to replace the caster wheels on MiR250. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/).

---

 * One or more caster wheels have flat spots on them.
 * Replace all four caster wheels. See How to replace the caster wheels on MiR250. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/).

---

 * One or more caster wheels are below 122 mm in diameter. For cleanroom wheels, the minimum diameter is 123 mm.
 * Replace all four caster wheels. See How to replace the caster wheels on MiR250. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/).
