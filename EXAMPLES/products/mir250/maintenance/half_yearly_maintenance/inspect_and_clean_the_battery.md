# Inspect and clean the battery {% tag="inspect_and_clean_the_battery" %}

<image: "mir250\_maintenance\_inspect\_battery.png">

## Purpose

To ensure that any potential bulging, leaking of electrolytes, or other signs that the battery is starting to fail is discovered before it happens.

Frequency: **half-yearly**

To complete this task, the robot does not need to be powered.

Turn off the robot, and disconnect the battery.

  * When performing maintenance on robots, you risk electrical shock from electrical wires and connectors if internal components are exposed, crushing, collision, or pinching from unexpected movements.

## Procedure for removing the battery

  1. Push the two buttons on the rear cover, and remove the cover from the robot.

<image: "rear\_press\_buttons.png"> <image: "rear\_remove.png">

  2. Disconnect the battery—see [Disconnecting the battery](../How to disconnect the battery.htm).

  3. Enable fast swap, if you have not already enabled this. The battery cannot be removed before fast swap has been enabled.

     1. Unscrew the two small screws mounting the status light bracket to the battery plate. Use a T20 bit.

<image: "fast\_swap\_1\_screws.png">

     2. Unscrew the two bolts and washers keeping the fast-swap levers raised. Use a 10 mm hex socket for the bolts and a 10 mm hex wrench to keep the nuts in place.

<image: "fast\_swap\_2\_washers\_screws.png">

     3. Pull the status light bracket down, and fasten the two levers to the Battery lever using the same two bolts, washers, and nuts.

<image: "fast\_swap\_3\_move\_status\_light\_bar.png">

<image: "fast\_swap\_4\_attach\_levers.png">

     4. Remove the battery from the robot.

<image: "pull\_out\_battery\_250\_mkII.png">

     5. Visually inspect the battery that all surfaces on the battery enclosure are even with no bulges.

     6. Check and verify that the identification sticker or QR code on the battery is intact and readable.

     7. Clean the battery with a dry and soft cloth. Do no use any solvents or abrasives.

## Pass criteria <image: "mmg\_pass.svg">

  * The battery is clean.

  * All surfaces of the battery are uniform and have no signs of bulges or deformities.

## Remediation

{% table %}

 * Issue
 * Remedial action
 ---
 * The battery is bulging or the surfaces of the battery is otherwise uneven.
 * Replace the battery. See How to replace the battery on MiR250, MiR600, and MiR1350. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/documentation/how-to-replace/how-to-replace-the-battery-on-mir250-mir600-and-mir1350/).

---

 * The battery is leaking electrolytes.
 * Replace the battery. See How to replace the battery on MiR250, MiR600, and MiR1350. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/documentation/how-to-replace/how-to-replace-the-battery-on-mir250-mir600-and-mir1350/).
