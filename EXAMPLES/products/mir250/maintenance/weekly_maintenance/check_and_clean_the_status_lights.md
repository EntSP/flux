# Check and clean the status lights {% tag="check_and_clean_the_status_lights" %}

<image: "mir250\_base\_robot\_inspect\_status\_light.png">

## Purpose

To ensure that the status of the robot is easily distinguishable and that it can be identified from all four sides. Dirty status lights can also sometimes cause false Protective stops because it reflects light into the safety laser scanners.

Frequency: **weekly**

To complete this task, the battery must be connected, and the robot must be turned on.

When performing maintenance, you risk electrical shock from exposed electrical wires, and crushing, collision, or pinching from unexpected movement.

  * Be careful of exposed electrical wires.

  * Avoid inserting your hands between actuators or other moving parts of the robot.

  * Make sure the drive wheels are not in contact with the ground—see [Lifting the robot](../How to lift the robot.htm).

## Procedure

  1. Visually inspect the status lights on the robot by looking up from below it—you can see the status light LED band inside the middle part of the front, rear, and side compartment covers.

  2. Verify that the status light LED band is intact and that all LEDs are functioning on all four sides of the robot.

  3. Wipe down all four parts of the status light LED band with a microfiber cloth.

See a [video](https://vimeo.com/user99588074) of the process on the Mobile Industrial Robots profile on vimeo.com.

## Pass criteria <image: "mmg\_pass.svg">

  * The status light LED band is intact and functioning on all four sides of the robot.

  * The status light LED band is clean, evenly lit, and has no dead LEDs.
