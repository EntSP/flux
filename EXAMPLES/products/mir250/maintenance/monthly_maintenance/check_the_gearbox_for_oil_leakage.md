# Check the gearbox for oil leakage {% tag="check_the_gearbox_for_oil_leakage" %}

<image: "mir250\_inspect\_gearbox.png">

## Purpose

To ensure that any potential oil leakage from the gearbox and motor assembly is discovered as early as possible so that a replacement bogie can be ordered, and a replacement can be scheduled.

Wear gloves for this procedure.

If your robot is very dirty, wipe away any dirt or dust on the gearbox in order to identify potential oil leakage better.

## Procedure

  1. Remove the front and rear compartment covers.

     1. Remove the two screws in the front cover, and remove the cover from the robot. Use a T30 bit.

<image: "front\_unscrew.png"> <image: "front\_remove.png">

     2. Push the two buttons on the rear cover, and remove the cover from the robot.

<image: "rear\_press\_buttons.png"> <image: "rear\_remove.png">

  2. Remove the side compartment covers.

     1. Turn the two screws counterclockwise on the side cover, and remove the cover from the robot. Use a T30 bit.

<image: "side\_unscrew.png">

  3. Use a paper towel to wipe the entire underside of the gearbox and motor assembly to check for any possible oil leakage.

  4. Repeat step 3 for the other gearbox and motor assembly.

## Pass criteria <image: "mmg\_pass.svg">

  * No oil leaks or oil residue underneath the gearbox and motor assembly.

## Remediation

{% table %}

 * Issue
 * Remedial action
 ---
 * Oil is leaking from the gearbox and motor assembly.
 * The entire bogie must be replaced. See How to replace the bogie with motor on MiR250. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/).

{% /table %}
