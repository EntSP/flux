# Inspect and clean the charging pads and broom {% tag="inspect_and_clean_the_charging_pads_and_broom" %}

<image: "mir250\_maintenance\_inspect\_charging\_pads.png">

## Purpose

To ensure that the robot will make proper contact with the charging station. Over time, the copper profiles on the charging pads on both robots and chargers will naturally experience oxidation or corrosion, which may impact charging performance negatively.

Frequency: **monthly**

To complete this task, the robot does not need to be powered.

Turn off the robot, and disconnect the battery.

* When performing maintenance on robots, you risk electrical shock from electrical wires and connectors if internal components are exposed, crushing, collision, or pinching from unexpected movements.

## Procedures for checking the charging pads

1. Reach underneath the front of the robot with your hand and verify that all four charging pads can move freely. Push them upwards to actuate.

2. Visually inspect each charging pad for oxidation. It is recommended to use a mirror to more easily be able to look up under the robot.

<image: "mir250\_maintenance\_mirror\_charging\_pads.png">

3. Inspect the bolts on the charging pads, and ensure that all are fastened correctly.

4. Visually inspect the broom in front of the charging pads. Make sure that it is free of dust, dirt or any entangled objects, and that no parts of the broom are damaged or missing.

## Procedure for cleaning the charging pads

The charging pads can be cleaned in several different ways. MiR recommends using option 1 below, but alternative options 2 and 3 are considered viable also.

## Option 1

1. Clean the charging pads with a piece of cloth and acetic acid (32%).

2. Visually inspect that any oxidation has been removed.

3. Rinse the charging pads with a clean cloth and some deionized water.

## Option 2

1. Make a solution with a 1:1 solution of citric acid or household vinegar and table salt (NaCl).

2. Apply the solution to the charging pads, and let it sit for 8-15 minutes.

3. Remove the solution with a piece of cloth.

4. Rinse the charging pads with a clean cloth and some deionized water.

5. Dry off the charging pads with some paper towel.

## Option 3

1. Sand off the oxidation using grit 3 000 sandpaper or finer.

2. Wipe away any sanding dust or residue with a clean cloth.

See a [video](https://vimeo.com/896181809) of the process on the Mobile Industrial Robots profile on vimeo.com.

## Pass criteria <image: "mmg\_pass.svg">

* All four charging pads on the robot are able to engage and move freely when pushed.

* There is no visible damage to any of the charging pads, for example, a loose spring.

* The charging pads are clean and do not have a layer of oxidation or corrosion on them.

* The broom in front of the charging pads is clean and not damaged.

## (Optional) Clean charging stations

You can clean the pads on charging stations in the same way as the charging pads on the robot.

<image: "maintenance\_charger\_charging\_pads.png">

Disconnect the charging station from the mains outlet before performing any cleaning tasks on the charging pads.
