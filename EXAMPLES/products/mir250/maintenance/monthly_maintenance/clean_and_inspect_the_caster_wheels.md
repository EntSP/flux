# Clean and inspect the caster wheels {% tag="clean_and_inspect_the_caster_wheels" %}

<image: "mir250\_maintenance\_caster\_wheels.png">

## Purpose

The caster wheels can move freely, ensuring that the robot drives as intended, stays accurately localized, does not report skidding or stall errors, and drives straight and stable.

Frequency: **monthly**

To complete this task, the robot does not need to be powered.

Turn off the robot, and disconnect the battery.

  * When performing maintenance on robots, you risk electrical shock from electrical wires and connectors if internal components are exposed, crushing, collision, or pinching from unexpected movements.

## Procedure

  1. Remove the side covers to properly access the wheels for cleaning.

  2. Lift the robot onto support blocks—see [Lifting the robot](../How to lift the robot.htm).

  3. Remove dirt with a damp cloth.

  4. Inspect all four caster wheels for debris or entangled objects that are stuck in the caster wheel mount.

  5. Verify that all four caster wheels can spin freely on both the wheel bracket and the wheels themselves, and you do not hear grinding noises when turning the wheels.

  6. Verify that all four caster wheels can spin freely without any play inside the wheel brace. Note that the caster wheel brace has a little play in the way it is attached to the robot frame. This is intended design.

  7. Verify that none of the bolts or screws are loose.

See a [video](https://vimeo.com/896453650) of the process on the Mobile Industrial Robots profile on vimeo.com.

## Pass criteria <image: "mmg\_pass.svg">

  * There is no grinding noises when turning the caster wheel.

  * The caster wheel and wheel bracket can spin freely, and there is no debris or items stuck in the caster wheel or caster wheel brace.

  * The nuts and bolts of the caster wheels are all securely fastened.

## Remediation

{% table %}

 * Issue
 * Remedial action
 ---
 * There are grinding noises or the caster wheel is spinning unevenly when the caster wheel is rotated.
 * Replace all caster wheels at the same time to ensure that the robot is level. See How to replace the caster wheels on MiR250. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/).

---

 * One or more bolts or nuts are loose.
 * Retighten the loose bolts or nuts using the appropriate size tools as listed in How to replace the caster wheels on MiR250. You can find this guide on [MiR Support Portal](https://supportportal.mobile-industrial-robots.com/).

{% /table %}
