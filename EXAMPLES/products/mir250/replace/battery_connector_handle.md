# Battery connector handle {% tag="replace_battery_connector_handle_mir250" %}


<image: "battery\_connector\_handle.png">

Failure to use appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) can result in serious injury.

Determine relevant and necessary PPE based on the task, environment, and experience of personnel. The following is a list of common PPE you must consider:

  * Safety shoes to protect from impact or puncture hazards.

  * Safety glasses and face shield to protect from flying debris or chemical agents.

  * Hard hat to protect from falling or flying objects.

  * Gloves to protect against sharp objects, electrical hazards, chemical agents, and extreme temperatures.

  * Ear plugs or earmuffs to protect from high noise hazards.

  * Dust mask or respirator to protect from inhaling chemical agents or other harmful airborne hazards.

Electrical components can be damaged by electrostatic discharge. Consider moving the robot to an ESD protected area and using ESD protected clothing and tools.

## Things you will need

  * A 10 mm hex screwdriver or wrench

  * A T30 torque wrench

## Kit contents

  * 1 × fast swap assembly

The part is delivered assembled.

## Before replacing the battery connector handle

  1. Turn off the robot by pressing and holding the Power button for three seconds.

## Replacing the battery connector handle

  1. Remove the rear cover by pressing the two white buttons and pulling off the cover. Un-click the two bottom corners first and then the two top corners.

<image: "rear\_cover\_buttons\_2.png"> <image: "rear\_cover\_remove\_2.png">

  2. Turn the Battery lever lock clockwise to unlock the battery connector.

<image: "unlock\_battery\_connected.png">

  3. Unplug the status light cable.

<image: "250\_unplug\_status\_light.png">

  4. Remove the battery connector by unscrewing the two M6 lock nuts securing the connector to the robot. Use a 10 mm hex wrench. Save the battery connector.

<image: "remove\_battery\_connector.png">

  5. Remove the fast swap assembly by unscrewing the four outer screws that fasten it to the robot. Use a T30 bit.

<image: "remove\_battery\_plate.png">

  6. Mount the new fast swap assembly with the new battery connector handle. Use the four outer screws to fasten it to the robot. Tighten these screws to 8 Nm.

<image: "mount\_battery\_plate.png">

  7. Re-mount the old battery connector.

  8. Plug in the status light cable.

  9. Pull the Battery lever up, and turn the Battery lever lock counterclockwise to lock the connector in place.

  10. Reattach the rear cover.
